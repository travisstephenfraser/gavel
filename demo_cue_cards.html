AI coding is exploding, especially among vibe-coders and non-engineers. They can generate code fast, but verification is still manual. Gavel is a quality gate for AI-generated code. It runs independent multi-model audits, reconciles confidence, and generates executable remediation. In short: evaluate, fix, and re-evaluate in one closed loop.

Slide 4 0:55-1:05
Open app, paste sql_injection.py, click Evaluate.

This sample contains SQL injection through string interpolation in a database query. Let's run it through Gavel.

Slide 5 1:05-1:30
Show scorecard and dimension cards.

Gavel scores four dimensions: correctness, security, readability, and robustness, using two independent models in parallel. Confidence and agreement show where verdicts are strong. In this case, security is clearly weak and flagged with high confidence.

Slide 6 1:30-1:50
Scroll to Remediation Report.

Now Gavel synthesizes findings into an actionable remediation report: severity, line context, and specific fix instructions the coding agent can execute.

Slide 7-8 1:50-2:30
Click Fix & Re-evaluate, then Re-evaluate Fixed Code; show delta.

This is the closed loop. Gavel applies staged autofix from remediation, compile-checks output, and pre-fills updated code. After re-evaluation, we get before-and-after deltas per dimension and an improved overall score. No human translation step between diagnosis and agent action.

Slide 9-10 2:30-3:00
Show market + close slide.

Our wedge is the fast-growing segment of new AI coders who can generate code, but cannot build robust verification stacks. Most tools stop at dashboards. Gavel closes the loop: evaluate, remediate, fix, re-evaluate. Built with coding agents, evaluates coding agents, and closes the loop.
